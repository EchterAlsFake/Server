Provision of the Website and Server Logs

This website is operated on my own server (a laptop) in a private residence in Germany. The operating system used is Arch Linux with a Flask web server. Access is provided via a reverse proxy (NGINX) with HTTPS encryption (TLS certificate), so that your connection data is transmitted confidentially and securely. No external hosting provider is involved; all data initially remains on my self-managed server.

When you visit the website, certain access data is processed temporarily for technical reasons (e.g., IP address and browser information) in order to deliver the website’s content to you. However, I refrain from the usual storage of IP addresses or other personal data in log files. No IP addresses are stored permanently. The server does not log individually which user accessed which pages. Instead, it only records aggregated statistics—especially the number of requests to the server and the data volume (in megabytes) of inbound and outbound traffic. This information is used exclusively to monitor server operation and bandwidth usage (e.g., to detect technical problems or unusually high traffic) and does not contain any personal individual details.

The temporary processing of IP addresses and technical data is necessary to deliver the website to your device and to ensure the security and stability of my offering. The legal basis for this is my legitimate interest in a secure and functional operation of the website (Article 6(1)(f) GDPR). As mentioned, only anonymized usage statistics without personal reference are stored permanently. These statistics are not combined with other data.

Use of Cloudflare (DNS & Protection Services)

I use the services of Cloudflare, Inc. (101 Townsend St., San Francisco, CA 94107, USA) in the free plan to provide my website faster and more securely. Cloudflare acts as the DNS provider for domain resolution and functions as a reverse proxy between you and my server. Among other things, Cloudflare provides an IPv4-to-IPv6 tunnel (so that my site is also reachable via IPv4) and offers DDoS protection and bot protection for my website. All requests from your browser to my website initially pass through Cloudflare servers distributed worldwide. Cloudflare filters harmful requests (e.g., from malicious bots) and forwards legitimate requests to my origin server. This significantly increases the security of my website and can also improve loading speed, as Cloudflare may cache content and deliver it from a server near you.

Processing by Cloudflare

By using Cloudflare, personal data such as your IP address and other connection data is technically processed and temporarily stored by Cloudflare. Cloudflare uses this information in particular to defend against attacks and to ensure website availability. Since Cloudflare’s server infrastructure is globally distributed, Cloudflare may also process this data worldwide. Cloudflare, Inc. is a U.S. company; data of website visitors may therefore be transferred to the USA. Cloudflare has, however, assured that it processes personal data in compliance with the GDPR. I have concluded a data processing agreement with Cloudflare in accordance with Article 28 GDPR, under which Cloudflare may process the data of my website visitors only on my instructions and for the purposes described here.

Cookies used by Cloudflare

This website itself does not use its own cookies (see the section “Cookies, Analytics Services and External Content” below). However, as part of its protection functions, Cloudflare may set a technically necessary cookie on your device, e.g., to distinguish legitimate visitors from bots. This cookie—called __cf_bm—is used for bot detection and is strictly required for the security functions; it does not contain directly personal data about you. The __cf_bm cookie has a short lifespan and is automatically deleted after 30 minutes of inactivity. Cloudflare does not use cookies for advertising or tracking purposes and does not perform cross-site user tracking via this cookie (a separate, non-linked cookie is set per website). Consent under Section 25 TTDSG is not required for this strictly necessary security cookie.

Legal basis

I use Cloudflare on the basis of Article 6(1)(f) GDPR. My legitimate interest is to protect my website against attacks, ensure high availability, and optimize loading times.

Please note that all data you transmit to my website (e.g., by accessing a page) inevitably also passes through Cloudflare as an intermediary. Cloudflare uses this data exclusively to fulfill the purposes stated above (protection and performance optimization) and not for its own advertising purposes. Cloudflare does not sell users’ personal data. Further information can be found in Cloudflare’s privacy policy on Cloudflare’s website.

Your Rights as a Data Subject

As a data subject affected by data processing, you have the following rights under the GDPR. You can exercise these rights against me at any time free of charge:

  • Right of access (Article 15 GDPR): You have the right to request confirmation as to whether I process personal data relating to you. If so, you have the right of access to this data and to further information, e.g., the purposes of processing, recipients, and the planned retention period.
  • Right to rectification (Article 16 GDPR): You have the right to request the correction of inaccurate personal data or completion of incomplete personal data without undue delay.
  • Right to erasure (Article 17 GDPR): You can request that I delete your personal data, provided the legal requirements are met. This is the case, for example, if the data is no longer necessary for the purposes for which it was collected, if you withdraw consent and there is no other legal basis, or if the data was processed unlawfully.
  • Right to restriction of processing (Article 18 GDPR): Under certain conditions, you have the right to request restriction of the processing of your data. For example, you may request restriction while I verify an alleged inaccuracy of your data.
  • Right to data portability (Article 20 GDPR): Where technically feasible, you have the right to receive data that I process automatically on the basis of your consent or for contract performance, in a commonly used, machine-readable format, or to have it transmitted to a third party.
  • Right to object (Article 21 GDPR): You may object at any time, on grounds relating to your particular situation, to processing based on legitimate interests. I will then stop processing your personal data unless I can demonstrate compelling legitimate grounds that override your interests, rights and freedoms, or the processing serves the establishment, exercise or defense of legal claims. Note: Your right to object also includes the right to object to direct marketing. In my case, however, I do not carry out any direct marketing using your data.
  • Right to withdraw consent (Article 7(3) GDPR): If you have given me consent to process personal data (e.g., when submitting feedback), you can withdraw this consent at any time with effect for the future. The withdrawal does not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out up to that point.
  • Right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority (Article 77 GDPR): You also have the right to lodge a complaint with a data protection supervisory authority about my processing of your personal data. You can do so, for example, with the authority in the EU member state of your residence or at the controller’s place of establishment. In Germany, this is in particular the state data protection authority of the federal state in which I have my residence/business establishment (here: Germany, State of Saxony-Anhalt).

To exercise your rights, you can contact me informally at any time (e.g., by email). You will find the contact details in the “Controller” section above and/or in the website’s legal notice.

Notice for Users from California (CCPA)

  • Right to Know: You have the right to know which categories of personal data I collect about you and for what purpose, as well as the right to access specific personal information I have stored about you (roughly corresponding to the right of access).
  • Right to Delete: You can request that I delete personal data I have collected from you, unless statutory exceptions apply (similar to the right to erasure).
  • Right to Opt-Out of Sale: The CCPA gives you the right to opt out of the sale of your personal information. Note: I do not sell any personal data to third parties—neither for payment nor in any other form. Your data is used exclusively as described in this policy.
  • Right to Non-Discrimination: You have the right not to be discriminated against for exercising your privacy rights. This means I will not provide you with a worse service or impose disadvantages on you because you exercise your rights (e.g., an access or deletion request).

To exercise your CCPA rights, you can contact me via the email address stated in the legal notice or above. Since— as described— I collect personal data only to a very limited extent, I expect few CCPA requests in practice. Nevertheless, I will process any verified request from California within the scope of statutory requirements. Please indicate in your request that it is a CCPA request and describe as precisely as possible which right you wish to exercise.
